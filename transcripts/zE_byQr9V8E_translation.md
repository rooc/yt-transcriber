**2:16** · The researchers believe that this brain replay helps a memory move from the hippocampus, the part of the brain where our more transient memories are, to areas of the neocortex.

**2:29** · That's where the more long-term lasting memories remain.

**2:33** · We already knew a little about this process of knowledge consolidation.

**2:37** · Sleep researchers have long known that sleeping well helps the brain process what it has learned and consolidate that learning.

**2:54** · What's new now is the power that small breaks on the go have over our memory and learning.

**3:35** · We need to give our brains the opportunity to process and store what we want to learn.

**3:40** · If you're a teacher or coach or teach any sport, the same applies to your students.

**3:46** · If you're going to teach them something new, plan those breaks during class or practice.

**5:04** · The brain needs to switch between focused and diffuse mode to learn effectively.
