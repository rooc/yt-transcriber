**0:08** Every time you type something like computeroy.com, youtube.com or any other URL in your browser, your browser doesn't connect directly to that name.
**0:18** The internet doesn't understand words, it only understands numbers, IP addresses like 142.250.180.110.
**0:28** The problem is that you can't memorize a huge list of numbers for everything you visit every day.
**0:33** And that's where a system that almost everyone uses without knowing it comes into play, DNS.
**0:38** DNS is what allows that understandable name you type in the browser to become the real IP to which your computer or mobile must connect for the page to load.
**0:53** In practice, DNS is behind almost everything you do on the internet.
**0:56** Every time you open a link, check email, use a streaming app, play online or even send a message, although you don't see it explicitly, it's working behind the scenes, translating that nice name into a machine address.
**1:11** Specifically, DNS are the initials of Domain Name System.
**1:17** When you type a domain in the browser, the DNS process seems almost instantaneous, but in reality it goes through several chained steps.
**1:25** Your DNS server comes into play, the one you have configured on the router, mobile, or PC.
**1:30** That server, which can be from the Operator, Google, Cloudflare or another company, searches within the Global DNS hierarchy.
**1:36** It can first ask top-level domain servers like those for .com, .es or .net and then the servers that directly control that specific domain.
**1:47** When it finds the IP, it returns it to your device and the browser uses it to connect to the web server and start downloading the page.
**1:57** In many cases, that IP is temporarily stored in cache, both on the DNS server and on your own device, so that if you return to the same page it doesn't have to repeat the whole process.
**2:09** That makes DNS queries normally take just a few milliseconds and seem almost invisible.
**2:14** But if the DNS server is slow, saturated, or poorly configured, that question-and-answer step can take several seconds, leaving the web loading before it starts downloading content.
**2:29** That's where changing DNS can make the difference.
**2:31** If you switch from a slow server to a faster one, that part of the process speeds up and browsing feels smoother, even if the bandwidth is the same.
**2:43** In most cases, when you buy a router or activate the wifi on your mobile, the system uses the default DNS set by the operator.
**2:50** You don't choose it, it simply comes pre-configured that way and all your DNS queries go through that operator's server, which is usually the company's own or some provider they have agreements with.
**3:04** That can ultimately bring several problems.
**3:06** If the server is saturated or not well optimized, name responses can be slower, which translates into pages taking longer to start loading, even if the page download goes just as fast once it starts.
**3:19** Additionally, some operators use DNS to filter or block content for parental controls, censorship, or internal policy.
**3:27** And they can analyze all your browsing habits through those queries, though not always in a very aggressive way.
**3:33** That's why, in many cases, it's recommended to stop using those default DNS and switch to public, fast, and well-maintained DNS servers like those from Cloudflare, Google DNS, Quad 9 or Open DNS.
**3:48** These services are designed to resolve millions of DNS queries quickly and securely and often noticeably improve the feeling of speed and stability of browsing.
**3:57** In practice, if what you're looking for is maximum speed and stability, many people choose Cloudflare or Google DNS.
**4:05** If, on the other hand, security is most important to you, Quad 9 and Open DNS are the most interesting.
**4:10** Again, to clarify, changing DNS doesn't increase your bandwidth, but it does improve page load time and the way the connection feels.
**4:21** And it may be just the adjustment that was missing for your wifi and mobile to feel faster than ever.
