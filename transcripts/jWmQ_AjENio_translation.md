**0:07** In today's digital world, every action you take online, whether browsing the internet, sharing posts on social media, or making electronic transactions
**0:18** you generate a large amount of data. However, have you ever wondered if that data could be used to obtain valuable information?
**0:27** Well, that's where OSINT becomes important. OSINT are the initials of Open Source Intelligence, which translated to Spanish is something like open-source intelligence
**0:36** or intelligence from open sources, an essential discipline in security and intelligence and data analysis. It's used to collect information about potential threats
**0:47** investigate crimes, identify vulnerabilities, and evaluate risks, and all thanks to the immense amount of existing information from public sources or other sources
**0:56** that are available to anyone. CI experts use it to discover information related to a company that could be vulnerable to cyberattacks
**1:06** and thus take preventive measures, although the entities themselves can also use it to obtain information about the competition, identify market opportunities
**1:16** and monitor their online reputation. Apart from these two main groups that use these techniques, governments are big consumers of OSINT
**1:26** using it for national security, the fight against terrorism, and political decision-making. OSINT techniques are currently divided into two main categories:
**1:34** passive and active. Passive collection involves obtaining information from public data sources without interacting directly with the target
**1:45** while active collection involves direct interaction, whether with employees or organizations, to thus gather more detailed data but with a greater risk of being detected.
**1:54** In Spain, there are various public information sources that can be used, such as public records, whether the Official State Gazette, the Mercantile Registry
**2:05** the National Institute of Statistics, social networks, search websites like Intel Techniques or OSINT Framework or osint.link, Maltego and SpiderFoot
**2:17** or even providers that collect and sell information. As you can see, and although there is a large amount of information available online, people and companies
**2:26** should always use it ethically. When using OSINT, professionals must ensure they do so for legitimate purposes and that the information is not used to exploit, harass, or harm others.
**2:37** Publicly available information is perfectly legal to access, analyze, and distribute. Just remember that attackers can also use it to promote illegal activities
**2:47** by filling websites with deceptive or malicious data, so you have to be very careful.
